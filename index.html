About Me

Early Life: I was born in Ireland and moved around frequently as a child, spending time living on opposite coasts of the country and overall living in about a dozen houses. As a result, I attended many different school and was never able to get comfortable in one location during that time. My family finally settled in one place within Dublin during my late teens though, after which point my school and social life became happier and more stable.

Education: In terms of post-secondary education, I've previously completed Post-Leaving Certificate courses in business and computer programming, during which I studied subjects such as business law and JavaScript. Currently, I'm a first year studying computer science at Dublin City University, where I study many interesting subjects such as web design and problem solving.

Work Experience

Shop Assistant, 2017: As a transition year student in secondary school, I found work experience as a shop assistant in a bookshop. I had a variety of duties, my primary ones were hoovering the carpets and making tea though. I also received on the job training about interfacting with customers and recommending books, which I found difficult since I didn't feel like talking to the customers and wanted them to leave me alone. Overall, it was a relaxing experience and I enjoyed it.

Database Designer, 2021: I held a temporary job for a few weeks of designing a Microsoft Access database for a dating agency. During this, I spoke with the management about Access Database's functionality and their requirements, then used their feedback to design a database. I implemented the design, populated it with content such as dating preferences, uploaded it to a secure back-end server and distributed front-end files to access it.
